## ⚡ Tech Hacks

- Used Excel and Power Query to clean and prepare 2,000+ records
- Handled missing values, removed duplicates, and standardized data
- Created calculated columns for Occupancy % and Available Capacity
- Developed DAX measures for KPI calculations and trend analysis
- Built a Calendar table for time-based analysis
- Designed interactive Power BI dashboards with KPI cards and slicers
- Used map visualization to analyze city-wise bike distribution
- Applied Top 5 filtering for better dashboard visualization

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze bike stand capacity across different locations
- Analyze bike availability based on banking feature status
- Compare available bikes across different stations
- Analyze bike availability and stand capacity across locations
- Evaluate monthly bike utilization trends
- Visualize bike availability using map-based analysis
- Analyze total bike distribution across different cities

📌 Insights

- Lyon has the highest bike-sharing infrastructure with 9,350 stands and 5,020 available bikes
- Bruxelles-Capitale contains 8,592 stands, 4,297 available bikes, and 3,532 total bikes
- Toulouse has 7,690 stands, 4,514 available bikes, and 2,686 bikes, making it one of the major bike-sharing cities
- Seville and Valence show balanced bike infrastructure and utilization across stations
- Lillestrom and Jcdecauxbike have very limited bike-sharing infrastructure with only 122 and 35 stands respectively
- Non-banking locations contain 18,332 bikes compared to only 1,314 bikes in banking locations
- Map visualization clearly highlights geographical differences in bike availability across cities
- Calculated columns, DAX measures, and Calendar table improved analytical depth and reporting

---

🧠 Conclusion

- The bike-sharing network is highly concentrated in major cities such as Lyon, Bruxelles-Capitale, and Toulouse
- Medium-sized cities maintain balanced infrastructure while smaller cities require additional expansion
- A significant imbalance exists between banking and non-banking locations in terms of bike availability
- Interactive Power BI dashboards provide valuable insights into station capacity, bike utilization, and infrastructure performance
- Overall, the analysis supports better urban mobility planning, resource allocation, and operational decision-making
