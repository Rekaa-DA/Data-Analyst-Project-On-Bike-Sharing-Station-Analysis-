## ⚡ Tech Hacks

- Cleaned and prepared **2,000+ records** using Excel and Power Query.
- Handled missing values, removed duplicate records, and standardized data.
- Created calculated columns including **Occupancy %** and **Available Capacity**.
- Developed multiple DAX measures for KPI calculations and trend analysis.
- Built a Calendar table for time-based analysis.
- Designed an interactive Power BI dashboard with KPI cards, slicers, and map visualization.
- Applied Top 5 filtering to improve dashboard readability.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze bike stand capacity across different cities.
- Evaluate bike availability based on banking and non-banking stations.
- Compare available bikes across different stations.
- Analyze bike availability and stand capacity across locations.
- Evaluate monthly bike utilization trends.
- Visualize bike availability using geographical map analysis.
- Analyze total bike distribution across different cities.

# 📌 Insights

- **Lyon** has the highest bike stand capacity with **9,350 stands** and **5,020 available bikes**, making it the largest bike-sharing network.
- **Bruxelles-Capitale** has **8,592 bike stands**, **4,297 available bikes**, and **3,532 total bikes**, indicating high operational efficiency.
- **Toulouse** recorded **7,690 bike stands**, **4,514 available bikes**, and **2,686 bikes**, making it one of the major bike-sharing cities.
- Smaller cities such as **Lillestrom (122 stands)** and **Jcdecauxbike (35 stands)** have very limited bike-sharing infrastructure.
- Non-banking stations contain **18,332 bikes**, while banking-enabled stations contain only **1,314 bikes**, indicating an uneven distribution.
- Interactive map visualization clearly highlights geographical differences in bike availability across cities.
- Calculated columns, DAX measures, and Calendar table improved analytical accuracy and dashboard performance.

---

# 🧠 Conclusion

- The analysis successfully identified infrastructure distribution and bike availability across multiple cities.
- Major cities such as **Lyon, Bruxelles-Capitale, and Toulouse** dominate the bike-sharing network with the highest stand capacity and bike availability.
- Medium-sized cities maintain balanced infrastructure, while smaller cities require further expansion.
- The significant gap between banking and non-banking stations indicates opportunities for better resource allocation.
- Interactive Power BI dashboards provide valuable insights that support urban mobility planning, infrastructure development, and operational decision-making.
